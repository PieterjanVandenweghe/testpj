Organizing the body part: <p> and <section>

If the body part of your concept is quite long, you can use paragraphs (<p> element) and sections (<section> element) to structure it.

<p>

If your topic includes more then one idea or several aspects of the same idea, it is best to create a separate paragraph for each idea or idea aspect. Because each paragraph starts on a new line, this division into paragraphs is a visual guideline for your readers: they know when you start a new idea or a new aspect of your idea.

This concept topic explains what an information type is. It includes a general description of the concept "information type" and an overview of the most important information types that are used within DITA. This topic is therefore composed of two paragraphs: one for the description and one for the overview:

<section>

Sections are quite similar to paragraphs, but are usually larger. Instead of grouping just one idea or one aspect of an idea, sections often group entire subsets of information. One section can be composed of several paragraphs. You can also add titles to sections, to point out the subject of a particular section.
